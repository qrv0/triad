# Manifest

## Purpose

This repository is the public structural and didactic home of Triad.

Triad is not presented here as a derivative of any prior discipline-specific formalism. Prior mathematical and scientific traditions may be used as interfaces, calibrations, or partial recoveries of the structure, but they are not the origin of Triad as such.

## Scope

This repository should contain:

- The Triad equation.
- Term-by-term explanation of the equation.
- The three inseparable principles: P1 oscillation, P2 self-reference with hierarchical memory, and P3 fluctuation-dissipation-locked coupling.
- The anti-collapse mechanism and its calibrated regimes.
- The structural-realist ontology: entities as calibrations of structure.
- The evaluation methodology appropriate to open, coupled, persistent systems.
- Cross-substrate interfaces and the ledger of calibrated correspondences.

This repository should not become:

- A general ML benchmark repository.
- An AGI experiment repository.
- A dumping ground for unreviewed outputs.
- A reduction of Triad to any pre-existing named framework.

## Rebuild Rule

Material from `archives/` returns to the root only if it has a defined role:

- `core`: required for the equation, principles, ontology, or method.
- `interface`: maps Triad into a substrate or discipline.
- `evidence`: supports a structural claim through derivation, data, or reproducible result.
- `implementation`: code needed to reproduce a core result.
- `result`: reviewed output or documented finding.
- `archive-only`: historically important but not part of the clean root.

## Authorship Note

Triad is treated here as an original human-directed recognition and formulation. AI tools may appear in the development history as instruments used in a long research and implementation process, but they do not replace authorship of the framework.
