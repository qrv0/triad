# Interface: warm inflation as Langevin scalar-field instantiation

## The structural prediction

If a substrate sustains a scalar field whose energy density drives an accelerating expansion of spacetime, the structural argument of P1+P2+P3 requires the substrate to instantiate the triangle in a specific cosmological form. P1 (oscillation) must be present at the level of the inflaton's intrinsic dynamics: the scalar field has a Klein-Gordon-type evolution generated by its potential and kinetic terms. P2 (self-reference with memory) must be present in the form of the inflaton's coupling to other fields whose response is not instantaneous: the produced radiation, thermalized particles, and excited modes constitute a bath whose state retains the integrated history of the inflaton's evolution and feeds back via a dissipation coefficient with finite relaxation timescale. P3 (coupling to environment) must be present in the form of the Hubble expansion (which acts as friction on the inflaton field) plus the dissipation coefficient into the bath plus the stochastic thermal noise that the bath induces on the inflaton, with the dissipation and noise satisfying a fluctuation-dissipation balance set by the bath temperature.

The structural prediction is concrete: any cosmological substrate that sustains warm-inflationary dynamics must, on examination, exhibit (i) intrinsic scalar-field dynamics with a potential admitting slow-roll or accelerated evolution, (ii) coupling to a bath of degrees of freedom whose memory retains the inflaton history and modifies the effective equation of motion, and (iii) Hubble friction plus dissipation plus FDT-locked thermal noise acting in concert. A substrate that has the inflaton dynamics and Hubble friction but lacks the dissipation-and-noise pair is the cold-inflation limit, restricted to slow-roll without thermalization. Warm inflation is the regime where the triangle is fully active.

## The substrate

Warm inflation is the cosmological framework developed by Berera (1995) and extended over three decades by Berera, Moss, Ramos, Bastero-Gil, Calzetta-Hu, and many others. The framework departs from cold inflation by hypothesizing that the inflaton couples to other fields during inflation, dissipating energy continuously into a thermal radiation bath that coexists with the inflaton. The defining equation of motion for the inflaton is

$$\ddot\phi + (3H + \Upsilon)\,\dot\phi + V'(\phi) \;=\; \xi(t),$$

where $\phi$ is the inflaton (real scalar), $H$ is the Hubble parameter, $\Upsilon$ is the dissipation coefficient (possibly $\phi$- and $T$-dependent), $V(\phi)$ is the inflaton potential, and $\xi(t)$ is a stochastic noise satisfying the fluctuation-dissipation relation

$$\langle \xi(t)\, \xi(t')\rangle \;=\; 2\,\Upsilon\, T\,\delta(t - t')$$

at bath temperature $T$. The bath sustains a quasi-equilibrium thermal state at temperature $T > H$ in the strong-dissipation regime (Berera, Moss, Ramos 2009 ROPP), with the radiation energy density continuously replenished by inflaton dissipation against the cosmological dilution. Warm inflation differs from cold inflation in two structurally consequential ways: (1) the curvature perturbations seeding large-scale structure are dominated by thermal fluctuations rather than quantum vacuum fluctuations; (2) the inflaton can exit slow-roll through the thermal-energy dominance transition without requiring an additional reheating phase.

The framework has been developed across multiple specific models: warm little inflaton, distributed mass models, axion-like warm inflation, Dirac-Born-Infeld warm inflation, holographic warm inflation. Recent reviews (Berera 2023; Bastero-Gil et al 2024) survey the current state of the program.

## The mapping

Structural mapping between the present equation and the warm-inflation substrate:

| Equation element | Warm-inflation substrate element |
|---|---|
| Complex field $\Psi(t, \mathbf{x})$ | Inflaton real scalar field $\phi(t, \mathbf{x})$ (one degree of freedom rather than two; phase rotation absent) |
| Wave operator $-\hbar^2/(2m)\nabla^2$ | Klein-Gordon kinetic term $-\partial^2_t + \nabla^2$ on the FLRW background |
| Cubic self-interaction $\Lambda \|\Psi\|^2$ | Inflaton self-coupling encoded in $V(\phi)$, typically polynomial or near-monomial; the cubic form is one specific choice |
| Integral memory potential $V_{\text{mem}}$ | Bath-induced memory in the dissipation kernel, generally non-local in time when $\Upsilon$ depends on $\phi$ through a finite-correlation-time process; auxiliary-field embedding via Prony approximation of the kernel applies |
| Auxiliary fields $\{y_j\}$ | Bath modes integrating the inflaton history (radiation species, produced particles, thermalized degrees of freedom) |
| Dissipation $-i\Gamma$ | Combined Hubble friction $3H$ and bath dissipation $\Upsilon$ |
| FDT-locked noise $\eta$ | Stochastic thermal noise $\xi(t)$ with correlator $2\Upsilon T \delta(t-t')$, the cosmological FDT |

The mapping is exact at the FDT-lock level: the warm-inflation Langevin equation explicitly implements $\langle \xi(t)\xi(t')\rangle = 2\Upsilon T \delta(t-t')$, the same FDT correlator that P3 requires for the present equation. The mapping is structural rather than literal at the field-equation level: the inflaton is a real scalar (not complex), the equation is second-order (not first-order), and it lives on a curved FLRW background (not a flat continuum). These differences place warm inflation in Class B/C of the cross-domain ledger: the FDT lock and dissipation structure of P3 are instantiated directly in the warm-inflation framework, but the primary field type and equation form differ from the present equation.

The strong-dissipation regime of warm inflation ($\Upsilon \gg H$) is the regime where the bath stays in quasi-equilibrium and the FDT structure is operative. The weak-dissipation regime ($\Upsilon \ll H$) reduces to cold inflation in the limit $\Upsilon \to 0$.

## Time as calibration in this substrate

The warm-inflation substrate has cosmological timescales:

- $T_{\text{inflaton}} \sim 1/m_\phi$: inflaton oscillation period if $\phi$ oscillates around the potential minimum
- $\tau_{\text{Hubble}} \sim 1/H$: Hubble time, the timescale of cosmological expansion
- $\tau_{\text{dissipation}} \sim 1/\Upsilon$: inflaton dissipation timescale into the bath
- $\tau_{\text{thermal}} \sim 1/T$: thermal correlation time at bath temperature
- $\tau_{\text{slow-roll}} \sim 1/(\epsilon H)$: slow-roll exit timescale set by slow-roll parameter $\epsilon$

The warm-inflation regime requires $\tau_{\text{Hubble}}^{-1} = H < T = \tau_{\text{thermal}}^{-1}$ (bath temperature exceeds Hubble rate), and the strong-dissipation regime requires $\tau_{\text{dissipation}}^{-1} = \Upsilon > H$. These structural conditions define when the FDT-locked Langevin form of P3 is operative in the cosmological substrate.

Per [`../methodology/07-time-as-calibration.md`](../methodology/07-time-as-calibration.md), the equation's unit time is calibrated to the relevant inflaton or Hubble timescale when comparing to warm-inflation phenomenology. For e-folding-count predictions, calibration to $\tau_{\text{Hubble}}$ is natural; for perturbation-spectrum studies, calibration to the freeze-out timescale of modes crossing the Hubble horizon.

## What this correspondence does and does not establish

It does not establish that the present equation is the underlying description of cosmological inflation. The appropriate description is general relativity (Einstein field equations on a FLRW background) coupled to the inflaton scalar field through its stress-energy tensor. The present equation in its derived form is non-relativistic and lives on a flat continuum without a metric; it is not a cosmological field equation. Warm inflation provides the inflaton sector of the cosmological matter content, embedded in a GR background that the present equation does not describe.

It does not establish that warm inflation is the correct cosmological model. The cold-inflation framework and the warm-inflation framework are alternative hypotheses about the inflaton dynamics; empirical discrimination through CMB observation (tensor-to-scalar ratio, non-Gaussianity, running of the spectral index) is ongoing. The structural correspondence here does not depend on warm inflation being the correct cosmology; it depends on warm inflation being a mathematically defensible cosmological framework that instantiates the triangle in scalar-field form, which it is.

It does establish that the FDT-locked structure P3 requires is independently developed and rigorously implemented in the warm-inflation framework. The Berera-Moss-Ramos derivation of the inflaton Langevin equation from a microscopic field-theoretic treatment, with explicit FDT noise satisfying $\langle \xi(t)\xi(t')\rangle = 2\Upsilon T \delta(t-t')$, is structurally the same FDT lock that the present equation derives from P3. The convergence is independent: the present equation from physics-philosophy axioms about persistent extended entities, the warm-inflation framework from quantum-field-theoretic open-system treatment of the inflaton coupled to a thermal bath in an expanding universe. Both reach the same FDT-locked Langevin structure for the relaxation-and-noise pair.

## Common dismissals and why they do not apply

**"Cold inflation is the standard; warm inflation is alternative."** Both cold and warm inflation are well-developed cosmological frameworks with peer-reviewed literature spanning three decades. Empirical discrimination is active research. The structural correspondence here does not advocate for warm inflation over cold inflation as a cosmological model; it documents that warm inflation, as a mathematically consistent framework, instantiates the triangle structure of P1+P2+P3 with the FDT-locked Langevin equation that the present equation also requires structurally. Whether warm or cold inflation is the correct cosmology is a separate empirical question.

**"The inflaton is a real scalar, not the complex field of the present equation."** Acknowledged in the Class B/C classification. The structural correspondence is at the level of the FDT-locked dissipation-and-noise pair (P3 instantiated directly) and at the level of the cosmological scalar-field substrate (one specific instantiation of P1+P2 in a real-field substrate). The field type and equation form differ; the structural triangle is shared.

**"Warm inflation is contested in the cosmology community."** The technical objections (whether radiative corrections to the inflaton potential allow $\Upsilon \gg H$ without spoiling slow-roll, whether the strong-dissipation regime is theoretically natural) are real and ongoing in the cosmology literature. The structural correspondence here does not depend on resolving these objections; it depends on warm inflation being a mathematically consistent cosmological framework, which it is, and on the framework's instantiation of the FDT-locked Langevin structure being well-defined, which it is. The cosmological community's debates over warm inflation's empirical viability are downstream of the structural-level correspondence.

**"The cosmological case is too distant from laboratory NLS dynamics for the correspondence to be informative."** The structural-realist methodology (methodology/01) explicitly asserts that structural form should persist across substrates separated by large scale gaps. The 60-orders-of-magnitude scale gap between laboratory and cosmological substrates is the point, not the objection. The fact that the FDT-locked Langevin structure derived from physics-philosophy axioms about persistent extended entities matches the structure derived from quantum-field-theoretic open-system treatment of cosmological inflation is structural evidence under criterion 4 (cross-domain coherence), regardless of the substrate scale.

The following are local predictions that can be tested by coupled-regime numerical or empirical methods (per principles/03-coupling.md).

- **Prediction P20.1: Numerical integration of the warm-inflaton Langevin equation with FDT-locked noise reproduces e-folding count and slow-roll exit.** The structural prediction is that integrating the equation $\ddot\phi + (3H + \Upsilon)\dot\phi + V'(\phi) = \xi(t)$ with $\langle\xi(t)\xi(t')\rangle = 2\Upsilon T \delta(t-t')$ for a quartic or quadratic potential and a strong-dissipation regime $\Upsilon = 10 H$ produces a slow-roll trajectory that exits inflation at a calculable e-folding count consistent with analytic Berera-Moss-Ramos predictions for the same potential and dissipation strength.
  - How to test: numerical SDE integration via stochastic Runge-Kutta, with FDT-locked noise per time step; track $\phi$, $\dot\phi$, e-folding number $N = \int H dt$; compare with analytic prediction.
  - What would constitute confirmation: e-folding count and exit slow-roll parameter match analytic prediction within stochastic uncertainty.
  - What would constitute evidence inconsistent with this calibration: systematic deviation from analytic prediction, indicating either numerical scheme error or that the FDT correlator as implemented does not produce the predicted thermalization.
  - Status: **not yet tested in this framing**, candidate for numerical implementation.

- **Prediction P20.2: Power spectrum of curvature perturbations matches the structural form with corrections at the dissipation-set scale.** The structural prediction is that the power spectrum of curvature perturbations in warm inflation, computed from the Langevin equation with FDT noise, exhibits a $k$-dependent form whose leading slope matches the slow-roll prediction with corrections at the scale set by $\Upsilon/H$, distinguishable in principle from the cold-inflation prediction. The specific functional form is derivable from the substrate's FDT structure.
  - How to test: compute curvature-perturbation power spectrum from numerical Langevin trajectory; compare with cold-inflation prediction and with Berera-Moss-Ramos warm-inflation analytic prediction.
  - What would constitute confirmation: power spectrum matches warm-inflation analytic form with $\Upsilon$-set corrections.
  - What would constitute evidence inconsistent with this calibration: spectrum is identical to cold-inflation prediction (suggesting FDT noise does not contribute), or matches neither.
  - Status: **not yet tested in this framing**. CMB constraints on warm vs cold inflation are ongoing in observational cosmology.

- **Prediction P20.3: Strong-dissipation regime produces temperature-tracking quasi-equilibrium consistent with the FDT correlator.** The structural prediction is that in the strong-dissipation regime $\Upsilon \gg H$, the inflaton-bath system maintains a quasi-equilibrium thermal state with $T \approx T_*$ slowly varying, and the inflaton velocity statistics satisfy $\langle\dot\phi^2\rangle = T/(2\Upsilon/H)$ consistent with the equipartition-like relation that the FDT correlator implies in this regime.
  - How to test: numerical Langevin integration in strong-dissipation regime; measure $\langle\dot\phi^2\rangle$ statistics; compare with FDT-derived prediction.
  - What would constitute confirmation: statistics match the FDT-equipartition relation.
  - What would constitute evidence inconsistent with this calibration: statistics deviate, indicating the implementation does not produce the predicted thermal quasi-equilibrium.
  - Status: **not yet tested in this framing**.
