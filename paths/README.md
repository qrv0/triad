# Paths

This folder contains five entry routes through the repository, each tailored to a specific reader background. The same body of work is approachable from different starting points; the path documents thread through the content in an order appropriate to each starting point.

| File | For readers who arrive |
|---|---|
| [`if-you-are-new.md`](if-you-are-new.md) | With no specific background. Curious. |
| [`if-you-are-from-physics.md`](if-you-are-from-physics.md) | Familiar with nonlinear dynamics, field theory, statistical mechanics. |
| [`if-you-are-from-ml.md`](if-you-are-from-ml.md) | Familiar with sequence models, attention, deep learning. |
| [`if-you-are-from-neuroscience.md`](if-you-are-from-neuroscience.md) | Familiar with neural oscillations, gamma rhythms, EEG, neural plasticity. |
| [`if-you-are-from-philosophy.md`](if-you-are-from-philosophy.md) | Familiar with structural realism, philosophy of science, methodological positions. |

## Why this folder exists

The standard practice in academic and machine-learning repositories is to assume that the reader's background matches the writer's. This works when the audience is homogeneous. The audience for this work is not homogeneous: the structural-realist position is most engaging to philosophers of science, the mathematical derivation is most engaging to physicists, the state space model correspondence is most engaging to machine learning researchers, and the gamma-entrainment interface is most engaging to neuroscientists. A repository written entirely in any one of those registers would be inaccessible to the other three.

The path documents are an attempt to solve this. They are not duplications of content; they are routes through the same content, calibrated to the prior knowledge and interests of different starting points. A philosopher entering through [`if-you-are-from-philosophy.md`](if-you-are-from-philosophy.md) reaches the same numerical results in [`../results/`](../results/) and the same cross-domain interfaces in [`../interfaces/`](../interfaces/) as a physicist entering through [`if-you-are-from-physics.md`](if-you-are-from-physics.md), but the order in which they reach those documents and the framing they encounter along the way is adapted to where they started.

A reader can switch paths mid-journey. A philosopher who finds the physics derivation more interesting than expected can move to the physics path partway through. A machine learning researcher who becomes curious about the methodological position can move to the philosophy path. The paths are routes, not silos.

## What the paths do not do

The paths do not lower the technical content. The derivation is the derivation; the methodology is the methodology; the cross-domain interfaces are what they are. The paths adjust the *order* and the *framing* of the encounter, not the underlying content.

The paths do not promise that the work will be easy for everyone. Some of the content is technical and requires concentrated reading regardless of which path the reader is on. The paths help a reader allocate that concentration appropriately to their background.
