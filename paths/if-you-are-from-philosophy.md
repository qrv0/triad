# Structural realism, operationalized

You are familiar with Worrall (1989), Ladyman & Ross (2007), Cartwright's disunity thesis, Duhem–Quine holism, and the limits of strict falsificationism. This work is best read as an attempt to operationalize the structural-realist position into a concrete worked example — one that brings the tension between P3 and falsificationist methodology into the open rather than resolving it quietly.

<div class="path-eq" markdown>
$$
\textbf{The load-bearing object of knowledge is the mathematical structure.} \\
\textbf{The cross-domain co-occurrence of the structure is the principal evidence for the structural-realist claim.}
$$
</div>

</div>

<div class="path-body" markdown>

## What this maps to in your area

The equation is derived from three structural axioms (P1 oscillation, P2 self-reference, P3 coupling). P3 — *coupling is the default; isolation is temporary* — is in direct structural tension with Popperian falsificationism, which presupposes the isolability of variables. A theory whose third axiom denies isolation cannot consistently be evaluated by a methodology that presupposes it; this motivates the six-criteria framework proposed in [`methodology/04-the-six-criteria.md`](../methodology/04-the-six-criteria.md). The cross-domain interfaces range from exact mathematical equivalence (no calibration: SSM, Kuramoto, other NLS systems) through calibration-dependent structural correspondence (gamma entrainment, archaeoacoustic resonance) to convergent-program correspondence (critical brain, Friston FEP, mechanistic interpretability), and the work is explicit about the evidentiary weight of each class.
