## What this maps to in your area

Under a dimensional identification (1 unit = 25 ms), the equation's absorption band lands at 20–200 Hz, encompassing the 40 Hz gamma frequency at which Iaccarino et al. (2016) document amyloid-β clearance and the OVERTURE Phase II trial reports slowed cognitive decline. A separate calibration ($dt = 9$ ms) places the equation's two principal vibrational modes at 66 Hz and 111 Hz — matching Cook, Pajot & Leuchter's (2008) finding that 110 Hz acoustic stimulation (specifically 110 Hz) produces left-temporal deactivation and prefrontal laterality shifts. The equation's slow memory mode ($\tau = 2$) and fast mode ($\tau = 0.1$) correspond structurally to cortical consolidation and hippocampal CA3, respectively — neither timescale was fit to biological data.
